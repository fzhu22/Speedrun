# Raw research — Foundations lane (LS techniques as implementable features: design parameters + boundary conditions)

### The Generation Effect (Slamecka & Graf, 1978)
Discipline: retrieval/generation; Stance: foundation; Link: https://andymatuschak.org/prompts/Slamecka1978.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: generated items remembered better than read items across cued/uncued recognition, free + cued recall, confidence; persisted across encoding rules, timing, test-info presence; under recognition the effect was response-item specific (not under cued recall); a memory phenomenon, mechanism unsettled.

### Test-Enhanced Learning (Roediger & Karpicke, 2006)
Discipline: retrieval/free recall; Stance: foundation; Link: http://psychnet.wustl.edu/memory/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Roediger-Karpicke-2006_PsychSci-1.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: restudy beat testing on a 5-min final test but testing produced substantially greater retention at 2 days / 1 week; restudy raised confidence even when delayed retention was worse; free-recall WITHOUT feedback produced large delayed gains.

### The Critical Importance of Retrieval (Karpicke & Roediger, 2008)
Discipline: retrieval scheduling / continued retrieval after success; Stance: foundation; Link: http://psychnet.wustl.edu/memory/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Karpicke-Roediger-2008_Sci.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: after one correct production, more STUDY had no effect on 1-week recall but more TESTING had a large effect; dropping items after one success greatly reduced retention; predictions uncorrelated with performance. [feature implication: don't retire a card after one correct answer]

### Retrieval Practice > Concept Mapping (Karpicke & Blunt, 2011)
Discipline: retrieval / meaningful learning; Stance: foundation; Link: https://learninglab.psych.purdue.edu/downloads/2011/2011_Karpicke_Blunt_Science.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: retrieval practice produced greater 1-week learning than elaborative concept mapping, on verbatim AND inference questions, even when the final task was making a concept map; students mispredicted mapping would win.

### Test-Potentiated Learning (Arnold & McDermott, 2012)
Discipline: test-potentiated new learning; Stance: foundation; Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3764602/
DOK 1 - Facts: retrieval enhances later memory directly AND enhances the benefit of later restudy (test-potentiated learning); even UNSUCCESSFUL retrieval attempts improved subsequent restudy. [feature implication: a failed attempt + feedback can be productive]

### Feedback with Multiple-Choice (Butler & Roediger, 2008)
Discipline: retrieval / feedback / MC boundary; Stance: foundation; Link: http://psychnet.wustl.edu/memory/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Butler-Roediger-2008_MemCog.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: MC testing can boost retention BUT exposes learners to lures and selecting a lure can create false knowledge; both immediate and delayed feedback increased correct delayed recall AND reduced lure intrusions; "provide feedback when using MC." [feature implication: MCAT-style MC cards REQUIRE feedback]

### Effective Learning Techniques utility review (Dunlosky et al., 2013)
Discipline: technique utility; Stance: foundation; Link: https://acs.ist.psu.edu/ist521/dunloskyRMNW13.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: practice testing + distributed practice = HIGH utility; elaborative interrogation, self-explanation, interleaved practice = MODERATE utility; techniques rated on generalization across conditions/learners/materials/criterion tasks.

### Self-Explanations (Chi et al., 1989)
Discipline: self-explanation / worked examples; Stance: foundation; Link: https://doi.org/10.1207/s15516709cog1302_1
DOK 1 - Facts: "Good" students generated many explanations relating example actions to text principles, guided by accurate self-monitoring; "Poor" students gave insufficient explanations, monitored inaccurately, leaned on examples; successful self-explanation produced example-independent, principle-level knowledge. [feature implication: self-explanation must be principle-directed + monitored]

### Dual Coding (Paivio, 1971)
Discipline: dual coding; Stance: foundation; Link: https://books.google.com/books/about/Imagery_and_Verbal_Processes.html?id=xmB9AAAAMAAJ
DOK 1 - Facts: two interconnected systems (verbal + nonverbal imagery); referential connections let one activate the other; double-encoding raises recall via two retrieval routes. [boundary: pair verbal+visual when BOTH meaningful, not decoration]

### Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning (Mayer, 2005; 2023 review)
Discipline: multimedia / dual-channel design; Stance: foundation; Link: https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/cambridge-handbook-of-multimedia-learning/cognitive-theory-of-multimedia-learning/A49922ACB5BC6A37DDCCE4131AC217E5 ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10648-023-09842-1
DOK 1 - Facts: dual channels, limited capacity, active processing; meaningful learning = select/organize/integrate words+images with prior knowledge; extraneous processing reduces capacity for essential+generative processing. [boundary: media must REDUCE extraneous load]

### Contextual Interference (Battig, 1972; Shea & Morgan, 1979; 2024 motor meta-analysis)
Discipline: contextual interference / variability; Stance: foundation; Link: https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED062661.pdf ; https://doi.org/10.1037/0278-7393.5.2.179 ; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11237090/
DOK 1 - Facts: greater interference during learning can improve later retention/transfer; random (high-interference) practice beat blocked on retention + transfer (esp. the most complex transfer task) when conditions changed; "random" used interchangeably with "interleaved"; Battig explicitly flagged the need to test applied-setting generalization. [boundary: high CI hurts acquisition-phase performance; motor→reasoning transfer not settled]

### Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller, 1988; van Merriënboer & Sweller review 2020)
Discipline: cognitive load; Stance: foundation; Link: https://andymatuschak.org/files/papers/Sweller%20-%201988%20-%20Cognitive%20load%20during%20problem%20solving.pdf ; https://repub.eur.nl/pub/128824/Repub_128824_O-A.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: schemas distinguish experts/novices; means-ends problem solving imposes heavy load that competes with schema acquisition; novices learn more from worked examples than equivalent problem solving (worked-example effect); intrinsic/extraneous/germane load. [boundary: difficulty that induces search can REDUCE learning]

### Worked examples + fading + self-explanation prompts (Atkinson, Renkl & Merrill, 2003)
Discipline: worked examples / fading; Stance: foundation; Link: https://mrbartonmaths.com/resourcesnew/8.%20Research/Making%20the%20most%20of%20examples/Fading%20out%20and%20Prompts.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: fading worked steps reliably fosters NEAR transfer but NOT far transfer alone; adding principle-identification self-explanation prompts produced medium-large effects on near AND far transfer, with no extra time on task. [feature implication: fade support + prompt for the principle]

### Expertise Reversal (Kalyuga, 2007; Tetzlaff et al. 2025 meta-analysis)
Discipline: adaptivity / expertise reversal; Stance: foundation; Link: https://www.uky.edu/~gmswan3/EDC608/Kalyuga2007_Article_ExpertiseReversalEffectAndItsI.pdf ; https://doi.org/10.1016/j.learninstruc.2025.102142
DOK 1 - Facts: technique effectiveness depends on prior knowledge; novice-helpful guidance becomes detrimental for experts (redundant load); 2025 meta (176 ES / 60 studies / 5,924 ppl): low-prior-knowledge learn better from HIGH assistance (d=0.505), high-prior from LOW assistance (d=−0.428); "giving assistance to novices appears more important than withholding from experts"; less clear for younger students + humanities/language. [feature implication: assistance must adapt to expertise]

### Spacing schedule design (Cepeda et al., 2008; 2025 classroom distributed-practice meta)
Discipline: spacing; Stance: foundation; Link: https://www.yorku.ca/ncepeda/publications/CVRWP2008.pdf ; https://www.mdpi.com/2076-328X/15/6/771
DOK 1 - Facts: optimal interstudy gap INCREASES with retention interval; optimal gap as % of RI declines from ~20-40% (1-week) to ~5-10% (1-year); a fixed "review in X days" is not optimal across exam horizons; 2025 classroom meta: distributed practice d=0.54 [0.31,0.77], larger with longer RI / higher ed level / fewer re-exposures. [feature implication: spacing should be exam-date / horizon aware]

### Metacognition architecture (Nelson & Narens, 1990; Koriat, 1997)
Discipline: metacognition; Stance: foundation; Link: https://sites.socsci.uci.edu/~lnarens/1990/Nelson%26Narens_Book_Chapter_1990.pdf ; https://iipdm.haifa.ac.il/images/publications/Asher_Koriat/1997-Koriat-CueUtilization%20JEPG.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: object-level vs meta-level; monitoring (up) + control (down); JOLs = likelihood-of-remembering judgments; JOLs use intrinsic/extrinsic/mnemonic cues; extrinsic factors (study time, repetition, duration) affected JOLs LESS than actual recall, and learners DISCOUNT repetition/duration; practice impaired calibration (more underconfidence) but improved resolution. [feature implication: confidence ratings alone won't change behavior unless tied to scheduling/feedback/strategy; don't let raw JOLs drive scheduling]

### Retrieval-Based Learning review (Karpicke, 2025); Carpenter/Pan/Butler 2022; 9-course STEM 2024; npj 2026 preview
Discipline: retrieval review / boundary; Stance: foundation; Link: https://learninglab.psych.purdue.edu/downloads/2025/2025_Karpicke_Retrieval_Based_Learning_Review.pdf ; https://sc-pan.github.io/pdf/NRP_2022.pdf ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40594-024-00468-5 ; https://preview-www.nature.com/articles/s41539-026-00400-2
DOK 1 - Facts: retrieval helps most when learners succeed with LITTLE support; expanding schedules > massed (add spacing while keeping success high); retrieval g=0.50–0.63 (Carpenter/Pan/Butler); learners UNDERUTILIZE retrieval/spacing (false beliefs); 9-course STEM: significant spacing effect in only 2/9 courses (generalizability unclear); 2026 preview: desirable-difficulty thresholds differ for learners with language disorder / WM limits. [boundary: enough support to succeed, little enough that retrieval occurs]

## Load-bearing facts for feature design
- Generation > reading across test types (Slamecka & Graf). Free recall w/o feedback > restudy on delayed tests (Roediger & Karpicke 2006).
- After one success, more testing (not study) drives 1-week retention; don't retire cards early (Karpicke & Roediger 2008).
- Retrieval works best with successful retrieval + little support (Karpicke 2025); expanding > massed.
- MC practice REQUIRES feedback or lures create false knowledge (Butler & Roediger 2008).
- Failed retrieval potentiates later restudy (Arnold & McDermott 2012).
- EI/self-explanation/interleaving = MODERATE utility; practice testing + spacing = HIGH (Dunlosky 2013).
- Effective self-explanation = principle-linked + monitored (Chi 1989); fading + principle prompts → near+far transfer, no extra time (Atkinson/Renkl 2003).
- Multimedia must manage dual channels/limited capacity; decoration consumes capacity (Mayer).
- Contextual interference improves retention/transfer but hurts acquisition; applied generalization not settled (Battig; Shea & Morgan).
- Worked examples beat problem solving for novices (Sweller); assistance must adapt to expertise (Kalyuga; Tetzlaff 2025 d=0.505 / −0.428).
- Optimal spacing depends on retention horizon; fixed intervals suboptimal (Cepeda 2008); distributed practice d=0.54 classroom (2025).
- Raw JOLs discount repetition/duration → don't drive scheduling by confidence alone (Koriat 1997).

## Flags (foundations)
- "Make Anki harder" is NOT a valid rule — sources distinguish productive difficulty from overload/unsupported search/redundant guidance.
- MC MCAT-style cards are double-edged: train format but encode misinformation without feedback + lure tracking.
- Interleaving/CI promising for transfer but domain/complexity-bounded; don't port motor-skill findings to MCAT reasoning without piloting.
- Worked-example/fading features need an EXPERTISE model (2025 expertise-reversal meta makes prior-knowledge adaptation load-bearing).
- Self-ratings shouldn't directly drive scheduling without calibration.
- Spaced retrieval not uniformly strong across STEM contexts (2/9 courses) → measure by content type/question family.
