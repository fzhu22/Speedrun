# Raw research — Adversary lane (which features BACKFIRE / are cargo-cult)

## LANE 1 — Gamification / streaks / points undermine intrinsic motivation
### Effects of Extrinsic Rewards on Intrinsic Motivation — meta-analysis (Deci, Koestner & Ryan, 1999)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/1999_DeciKoestnerRyan_Meta.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Across 128 studies, engagement-/completion-/performance-contingent rewards undermined free-choice intrinsic motivation: d = −0.40, −0.36, −0.28.
- Tangible + expected rewards undermined self-reported interest; the "overjustification effect."
- Tangible rewards more detrimental for children than college students.
- CONTRAST: positive verbal feedback ENHANCED free-choice behavior (d=+0.33) and interest (d=+0.31) — harm is reward-specific, not all feedback.

### Negative effects of gamification in education software (Almeida, Kalinowski et al., 2023)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0950584922002518
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Systematic mapping found 87 papers reporting undesired effects; badges/leaderboards/competitions/points most often cause negative effects.
- Most-cited negatives: lack of effect, worsened performance, motivational issues, lack of understanding, "gaming the system"/cheating.

### Negative Effects of Gamification — PRISMA review (2025)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://doi.org/10.1109/icca66035.2025.11431021
DOK 1 - Facts:
- PRISMA review (206 studies 2020–2025, 20 selected): negatives = performance pressure/psychological strain, superficial learning, motivational decline, emotional strain.
- Leaderboards/challenges/points/narratives/rewards can weaken autonomy/competence/relatedness if poorly designed; shift focus from learning to competition.

### Impact of gamification on academic performance: meta-analysis 2008–2023 (Huang et al., BJET 2024)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://doi.org/10.1111/bjet.13471
DOK 1 - Facts:
- The combination Levels+Badges+Leaderboards had a NEGATIVE effect on academic performance.
- Points and leaderboards may induce shame among lower-performing students.

### Exit Dark Patterns in Gamified Educational Apps (Univ. Waterloo CSL, 2026) [practitioner/qualitative]
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://csl.uwaterloo.ca/download/documents/reportsarticles/idc26a_sub2151_i6pdf;v1?attachment=1
DOK 1 - Facts:
- 41/50 family dyads (82%) reported anxiety/conflict when trying to pause/stop use.
- Streak continuity + loss-framing restructured motivation from "I want to learn" to "I must not lose my streak"; learners avoided difficult lessons to preserve streaks ("not quitting, not learning—just maintaining").

### Duolingo streak-anxiety analyses (My Senpai; DataField, 2024) [practitioner / secondary]
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://my-senpai.com/insights/why-people-quit-duolingo.html ; https://datafield.dev/algorithmic-addiction/part-03/chapter-16/case-study-01.html
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Streaks measure daily app-opens, not learning (a "200-day streak of 5-min autopilot" = 200 days real study).
- External rewards displace internal motivation; quitting happens all at once when the streak breaks.
- Cites that long-streak users report HIGHER anxiety than short/no-streak users; broken streaks durably suppress re-engagement (Silverman & Barasch, JCR 2023).

## LANE 2 — Interleaving backfires for verbal/expository/word material
### Similarity Matters: Interleaved Learning meta-analysis (Brunmair & Richter, 2019)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31556629/
DOK 1 - Facts:
- 59 studies/238 ES: blocking BEAT interleaving for words, g = −0.39 (interleaving actively harmful); expository texts non-significant; benefit concentrated in paintings (0.67), math (0.34).
- Meta-regression negative weights vs paintings: expository −0.56 (p=.001), words −0.48, math −0.43.
- Works only when material is more similar BETWEEN than WITHIN categories (confusable); "use with caution… especially expository texts and words."

### Why Do Learners (Under)Utilize Interleaving? (Ed. Psych. Review, 2024)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10648-024-09902-0
DOK 1 - Facts:
- "Overwhelming preference for blocking" when learning confusable categories; learners scarcely self-adopt interleaving (low metastrategic knowledge / perceived utility) → adherence cost if forced.

## LANE 3 — "Desirable difficulties" harm novices / high load / expertise reversal
### The Expertise Reversal Effect (Kalyuga, Ayres, Chandler & Sweller, 2003)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://rob.co.bb/the-expertise-reversal-effect-kalyuga-2003.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Techniques effective for novices "can lose their effectiveness and even have negative consequences" for experts (redundant guidance imposes WM load).

### Undesirable Difficulty Effects in High-Element-Interactivity Materials (Chen, Kalyuga & Sweller, 2018)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.01483/full
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Desirable difficulties "are not always obtained"; under high element interactivity, added difficulty "may be undesirable rather than desirable"; element interactivity depends on learner expertise.

### Expertise reversal — cognitive load explanation (Roodenrys et al., 2011)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://doi.org/10.1037/a0022243
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Replicated: additional text helped novices' retention/transfer, reversed for experts; explained by cognitive load, not motivation.

## LANE 4 — Dual coding misapplied: decorative/seductive images hurt
### Seductive detail effect meta-analysis (Rey, 2012)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1747938X12000413
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Small–medium NEGATIVE on retention, medium NEGATIVE on transfer; mechanisms: WM overload, attention distraction, schema interference, coherence disruption.

### Coherence principle / seductive details (Harp & Mayer 1998; Sundararajan & Adesope 2020)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://theeconomyofmeaning.com/2020/03/02/how-a-cartoon-in-a-textbook-can-hurt-learning-a-new-meta-analysis-of-the-seductive-details-effect/
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Interesting-but-irrelevant words/images/sounds lower recall and transfer; coherence principle = exclude extraneous material; seductive details at passage START interfere most.

## LANE 5 — Self-explanation / elaborative interrogation: shallow/null without scaffolding
### Improving Students' Learning With Effective Learning Techniques (Dunlosky et al., 2013)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1529100612453266
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Elaborative interrogation, self-explanation, interleaving rated only MODERATE utility (vs HIGH for practice testing + distributed practice).
- EI/SE "have not been adequately evaluated in educational contexts"; benefits "may be limited for learners with low domain knowledge."
- Self-explanation increases time demands 30–100%; untrained students paraphrase instead of explaining.

### Constraints on when self-explanation aids learning (Rittle-Johnson, Loehr & Durkin, 2017)
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://doi.org/10.3758/s13423-016-1079-5
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Self-explanation "can reduce recall of details and promote overgeneralization"; neutral/negative if the learner's methods are likely wrong; explanation prompts reduce attention to other info; instructional explanations can beat prompted self-explanation.

### Metacognitive Overload! (iSTART; McCarthy et al., 2018 — McNamara senior author) [citation-gate FIX: first author is McCarthy, not McNamara]
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40593-018-0164-5
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Metacognitive prompts had NO effect on comprehension and a DETRIMENTAL effect on self-explanation performance; "particularly damaging for less skilled readers"; authors cautioned other designers (the "removed in future versions" phrasing is unverified — soften).

## LANE 6 — Over-atomization / cloze culture trains recognition & cue-dependency

DOK 1 - Facts:
- JOLs made with the target present "instill a sense of competence during learning that proves unwarranted during testing" (cue–target vs a priori association dissociation).

### Self-Regulated Learning: Beliefs, Techniques, and Illusions (Bjork, Dunlosky & Kornell, 2013)

DOK 1 - Facts:

## LANE 8 — Knowledge-tracing / adaptive features unstable, barely beat baselines

Stance tag: adversary

DOK 1 - Facts:
- Properly-replicated BKT AUC 0.67→0.73 (Assistments); enhanced BKT "indistinguishable" from DKT; "shallow models like BKT can perform just as well" with more interpretability.

Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2101.08349
DOK 1 - Facts:

## LANE 11 — Anki's own failure modes (ease-hell, leeches, review-pile)
### Anki ease-hell, leeches & overwhelm (Anki manual + practitioner analyses) [practitioner + official]
Stance tag: adversary
Link: https://faqs.ankiweb.net/what-spaced-repetition-algorithm ; https://my-senpai.com/insights/why-people-quit-anki.html
DOK 1 - Facts:
- SM-2 ease "never decreased below 130%" → repeatedly-failed cards bottom out, become due too often ("ease hell"); FSRS mitigates this.
- Default leech threshold 8 lapses → auto-suspend; leeches "consume disproportionate review time relative to retention."
- Reviews compound (20 new/day → 100+ reviews/day within a month); missed days create a backlog "impossible to repay."
- Anki manual: above 90% desired retention "workload increases very quickly," above 97% "overwhelming."
- Overloaded/over-atomized cards violate the Minimum Information Principle and are the root cause of leeches (card-design failure, not discipline).

## Flags (adversary honesty caveats)
- Gamification meta-analyses are net-POSITIVE on average (Sailer & Homner 2019; Huang 2024) — the claim is narrower: specific elements (Levels+Badges+Leaderboards) and contingent/expected rewards backfire. Don't overstate to "all gamification harms."
- 2025 PLOS Digital Health found NO hallucinations in GPT-4 family-med MCQs (no mitigation, single domain) — so "LLM cards always hallucinate" is too strong; "require human/RAG verification or ship faulty items" is defensible.
- Choice-overload literature is contested/condition-dependent — frame bloat harm as conditional (high complexity, anxious users).
- Duolingo/Anki streak-anxiety sources are practitioner blogs (corroborated by Deci/Ryan + Silverman & Barasch JCR 2023) — citation gate should anchor on the peer-reviewed sources.
- The transfer-to-MCAT-passages claim has the THINNEST direct evidence (no source directly measures flashcard→novel CARS/applied performance) — load-bearing weak point; rests on parent's Pan & Rickard + med-ed rote-vs-conceptual studies.
