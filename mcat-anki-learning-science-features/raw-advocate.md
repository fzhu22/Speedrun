# Raw research — Advocate lane (strongest evidence FOR specific feature interventions)

### Inducing Self-Explanation: A Meta-Analysis (Bisra et al., 2018)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://gwern.net/doc/psychology/spaced-repetition/2018-bisra.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: 69 effect sizes / 64 reports / 5,917 ppl; prompted self-explanation g=.55 [.45,.65]; effective across problem solving, worked examples, text study; recommends computer-generated self-explanation prompts.

### Effective Learning Techniques utility review (Dunlosky et al., 2013)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://doi.org/10.1177/1529100612453266
DOK 1 - Facts: practice testing + distributed practice = HIGH utility; EI/self-explanation/interleaving = MODERATE; practice testing includes self-generated flashcards (retrieve + check).

### Meta-Analysis of Ten Learning Techniques (Donoghue & Hattie, 2021)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://doi.org/10.3389/feduc.2021.581216
DOK 1 - Facts: 242 studies / 1,619 effects / 169,179 ppl; overall d=0.56; distributed practice d=0.85, practice testing d=0.74, elaborative interrogation d=0.56, imagery d=0.56, self-explanation d=0.54; MOST outcomes were surface/factual (caution on deeper outcomes); feedback + near/far transfer + ability moderated.

### User-Generated > Premade Digital Flashcards (Pan, Zung, Imundo, Zhang, Qiu, 2023)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://doi.org/10.1037/mac0000083
DOK 1 - Facts: 6 experiments; 48-hr delay; user-generated improved memory d=0.45 [0.25,0.66] AND application-question performance d=0.29 [0.12,0.45]; 5/6 experiments favored user-generated. [feature implication: support card CREATION, not just consumption]

### Generation Effect meta-analysis (Bertsch et al., 2007)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17645161/
DOK 1 - Facts: 445 ES / 86 studies; overall generation effect .40 (~half SD favoring generation over reading); 11 moderators coded.

### Testing vs Restudy meta-analysis (Rowland, 2014)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://courseware.epfl.ch/assets/courseware/v1/.../Rowland2014-meta-analysis.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: 159 ES / 61 studies; testing effect mean g=0.54 UNWEIGHTED (median .55; headline WEIGHTED ES = g=0.50 — citation-gate clarification); 81% positive; CUED-recall initial tests g=0.61 > free recall g=0.29 / recognition g=0.29; WITHOUT feedback, effect near zero when initial performance ≤50% (g=0.03). [feature implication: cued production + feedback]

### Classroom Retrieval Practice (Agarwal, Nunes, Blunt, 2021; Roediger et al. 2011)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://pdf.poojaagarwal.com/Agarwal_etal_2021_EDPR.pdf ; https://pdf.retrievalpractice.org/guide/Roediger_Agarwal_etal_2011_JEPA.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: 50 classroom experiments, n=5,374; 57% medium/large benefits; 16 large (d>0.80), 12 medium, 18 small, 3 NEGATIVE; studies WITHOUT feedback mostly small/very small. Roediger 2011: low-stakes MC quizzing improved chapter + semester exams (incl. web self-quizzing).

### Feedback corrects metacognitive errors (Butler, Karpicke, Roediger, 2008)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://doi.org/10.1037/0278-7393.34.4.918
DOK 1 - Facts: feedback improved retention by correcting erroneous responses; DOUBLED retention of correct LOW-confidence responses vs no feedback; corrects both memory + metacognitive errors.

### AI metacognitive-calibration RCT (Lee et al., 2025)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://doi.org/10.1145/3706598.3713960
DOK 1 - Facts: RCT N=133; intervention = AI-predicted posttest grade + prompt to compare with student's own prediction; +8.9% learning gains (t=−2.384, p=.019); overconfident students +4.1% calibration (t=2.001, p=.049); learning behaviors mediated.

### Interleaving meta-analysis (Brunmair & Richter, 2019)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://www.psychologie.uni-wuerzburg.de/fileadmin/06020400/2019/Brunmair_Richter...pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: 59 studies/238 ES; overall g=0.42 [.34,.50]; paintings .67, photos .35, math .34 [.11,.57]; words BLOCKING advantage g=−0.39; stronger when materials more similar BETWEEN / less similar WITHIN / more complex; NO moderation by retention-vs-transfer (p=.45).

### Interleaved math practice (Taylor & Rohrer 2010; Rohrer et al. RCT 2020)
Stance: advocate; Link: http://uweb.cas.usf.edu/~drohrer/pdfs/Taylor&Rohrer2010ACP.pdf ; http://uweb.cas.usf.edu/~drohrer/pdfs/Rohrer_et_al_2020JEdPsych.pdf
DOK 1 - Facts: 4th-grade interleaved math more than DOUBLED test scores (77% vs 38%, d=1.21); interleaving impaired PRACTICE but improved DELAYED test. 7th-grade RCT: interleaved beat blocked d=0.83 [0.68,0.97]; positive for all 15 teachers (d=0.23–1.48).

### Adaptive scheduling (Eglington & Pavlik, 2020)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41539-020-00074-4
DOK 1 - Facts: model-scheduled adaptive practice → up to 40% more items recalled; OET 86/94 > OET 40/98/fixed S15; pseudo-R²=0.37; AUC=0.871.

### Personalized review (Lindsey, Shroyer, Pashler, Mozer, 2014)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://doi.org/10.1177/0956797613504302
DOK 1 - Facts: semester-long middle-school language course; cumulative post-semester exam; personalized review +16.5% retention over massed, +10.0% over one-size-fits-all spacing; combines individual-difference stats + memory theory.

### Spaced education in medicine (Kerfoot 2007 / 2009 / 2010)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17209889/ ; https://www.auajournals.org/doi/10.1016/j.juro.2009.02.024 ; https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jamcollsurg.2010.04.023
DOK 1 - Facts: weekly emailed case scenarios + questions; improved end-of-year scores (p<0.001), largest at 6-8 mo (d=1.01) / 9-11 mo (d=0.73); benefits retained 2 years later (70.2% vs 66.8%, d=0.35, p=0.03); spaced-ed with histopathology IMAGES + immediate feedback generated TRANSFER of diagnostic skills + long-term retention. [feature implication: spaced CASE/image questions with feedback transfer]

### Anki → medical-exam performance (Frappa et al. 2026 review [Med Sci Educ 36:1015–1025; mislabeled "Rana" in draft]; Deng 2015; Gilbert 2023; Winter 2025)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40670-026-02643-5 ; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4673073/ ; https://doi.org/10.1007/s40670-023-01826-8 ; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12357012/
DOK 1 - Facts:
- Frappa et al. 2026 review [citation-gate FIX: NOT "Rana"]: 11 studies; Lu et al. Anki users 241.1±13.2 vs non 235.5±17.7 (p=0.012), consistent users up to 248.7 (p<0.001); daily users Step 1 median 238 vs 233.5 (p=0.039); NO study randomized Anki use (observational).
- Deng 2015 (n=72): unique Anki cards seen independently predicted Step 1 (B=5.9e-4, p=0.024) — ~1 Step-1 point per ~1,700 unique cards; boards-style MCQs also predicted (p<0.001); test anxiety negative; Firecracker cards did not predict.
- Gilbert 2023 (130 M1; Anki training program): Anki users scored higher — Course I +6.4% (p<0.001), II +6.2%, III +7.0%, CBSE +12.9% (p=0.003); adjusted for MCAT percentile (ANCOVA).
- Winter 2025 (n=36): mature cards = interval >21 days; CBSE correlated with hours (p=0.013), matured cards (p=0.002), unsuspended (p=0.010); ≥9,390 mature cards → 71.56% vs 60.05% (p=0.002); mature cards predicted CBSE (β=0.649, p=0.026, R²=0.427).

### Visual/anatomy practice (Backhouse 2015 drawing; Styn 2023; Moro 2020 images; 2026 drawing)
Stance: advocate; Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26033842/ ; https://doi.org/10.1002/ase.2237 ; https://discovery.ucl.ac.uk/id/eprint/10102430/1/ase.1965.pdf ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10459-026-10530-2
DOK 1 - Facts: actual drawing of histology images improved retention at 4/6 weeks (n=384 RCT cross-over); tablet vs paper drawing no main difference but high implementation quality → tablet > paper (β=0.40, p=0.03); SBA questions WITH images scored higher than text-only (p<0.001, n=174); drawing improved spatial organization + monitoring accuracy but NOT recall of nonpictorial info. [feature implication: image/diagram + drawing has bounded, quality-dependent value]

## Concise summary (advocate)
- Self-explanation prompts g=.55 (Bisra 2018); software-generated prompts recommended.
- Practice testing d=0.74, distributed practice d=0.85 (Donoghue & Hattie 2021) — but mostly surface outcomes.
- User-generated > premade cards: memory d=0.45, application d=0.29 (Pan 2023).
- Generation effect .40 (Bertsch 2007); testing vs restudy g=0.54, cued > free/recognition, near-zero w/o feedback ≤50% (Rowland 2014).
- Classroom retrieval medium/large in 57% (Agarwal 2021); no-feedback mostly small.
- Feedback doubles retention of low-confidence-correct answers (Butler 2008).
- AI calibration feedback +8.9% learning / +4.1% calibration (Lee 2025).
- Interleaving g=0.42 overall (words −0.39); interleaved math d=0.83 RCT (Rohrer 2020).
- Adaptive spacing +40% recalled (Eglington & Pavlik 2020); personalized review +16.5%/+10% (Lindsey 2014).
- Spaced medical cases d=1.01, transfer of image-diagnosis skills, 2-yr retention (Kerfoot).
- Anki↔Step-1: ~1 point per ~1,700 cards (Deng); +6-13% course/CBSE (Gilbert); mature cards predict CBSE (Winter) — ALL observational.
- Images on anatomy questions help (Moro); drawing helps spatial/retention with bounded conditions.

## Flags (advocate)
- Direct Anki↔exam evidence is OBSERVATIONAL — the 2026 systematic review explicitly states NO included study randomized Anki use (selection/confounding: motivated students use Anki). Strong association, weak causation.
- Interleaving is feature-ready for CONFUSABLE categories / mixed problem selection but has a blocking advantage for word-based materials (g=−0.39) — targeting matters.
- Image occlusion specifically has LESS direct causal evidence than drawing/labeling/visual-question/spaced-case evidence.
- Self-explanation has strong general evidence but software/LLM-specific evidence is newer/less settled.
- Feedback helps but consumes time → prioritize short, targeted post-retrieval feedback.
- Donoghue & Hattie caution: most measured outcomes are surface/factual, not the deeper/relational outcomes the MCAT needs.
